COVID-Net

At DarwinAI, I have had the opportunity to work closely with Dr. Alexander Wong, and have assisted in the development of COVID-Net, which is a neural network that is capable of identifying the presence of pneumonic COVID-19 from a chest X-Ray.

This tool is able to discriminate well between other pneumonic infections and COVID-19. Additionally, in-conjunction with Gensynth's Inquire tool, insights can be obtained around the triggering information in the image.

I focused primarily on building transfer learning tools to demonstrate that a measure of severity can be obtained as well. A complete version of this is realized as Covid-Net-S, which can be found on the project's Github.

Frost-E-Bike

Made entirely of 6061-T6-grade Aluminum and with an IP-67 grade sealing, this ambitious final-year capstone project was the culmination of multiple attempts to build a fun way to get around campus no matter the season.

The bike has numerous additional features, including torque-vectored steering, heated handlegrips, turning signals, regenerative braking and pedalling, and over 2 kW of available AWD electric power!

Synthesizer

This was a fun project where I built a raspberry pi zero, a battery, a DAC and a tiny OLED screen into a USB-MIDI keyboard. On the software side it is running raspbian headless and uses PureData to handle the synthesis.

This little keyboard works really well as a sampler!
